2) Definitions (simple)

  • Food infection: Illness after eating live pathogens that multiply in the body (e.g., Salmonella).
  • Food intoxication: Illness from pre-formed toxin in food (e.g., Staph. toxin). (for contrast)
  • Food infestation: Contamination/damage by insects, mites, or rodents in stored foods (e.g., weevils in grains).

5) Quick Recognition (symptoms)

  • Fever + diarrhea → infection (e.g., Salmonella, Campylobacter).
  • Bloody diarrhea → think EHEC (E. coli O157:H7).
  • Pregnancy risk → Listeria (avoid high-risk RTE foods unless heated).
  • Jaundice weeks after eating → consider Hepatitis A.

10) Often-Missed Concepts (marks booster!)

  • Reheating ≠ fix infection risk: If toxin formed earlier, heating may not help; control time-temperature earlier.
  • Listeria & fridge: Can grow at ≤ 4 °C—RTE meats/soft cheeses are higher risk.
  • Water safety matters: Many infections begin with contaminated water used for washing/ice.
  • Low aw foods still spoil: Mites/insects thrive in dry stores—keep areas clean & sealed.
  • Stock rotation: Long storage ↑ infestation risk; use FIFO/FEFO and date labels.
  • Pest proofing: Small gaps allow rodents; install door sweeps & mesh screens.
  • Receivals check: Pests often arrive with the goods—inspect bags on delivery.
  • Allergens & pests: Insect fragments can trigger reactions—sieving & cleaning matter.
  • Waste removal: Spilled grain/sweet residues attract pests—clean immediately.
  • Records help: Temp logs, pest-trap logs, cleaning logs = traceability & quick fixes.
