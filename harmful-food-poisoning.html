2) What is “Food Poisoning”?

Food poisoning = illness after eating unsafe food. Three main paths:

  • Intoxication: eat a toxin already in food (e.g., Staph. toxin, botulinum toxin) → fast onset.
  • Infection: eat live pathogens that grow in gut (e.g., Salmonella, Listeria) → fever/diarrhea.
  • Toxico-infection: eat bacteria that make toxin after ingestion (e.g., Bacillus cereus diarrheal type).

5) Quick Recognition (symptoms)

  • Fast vomiting (1–6 h) → think Staph. toxin or B. cereus (emetic).
  • Neurologic signs (blurred vision, paralysis) → suspect botulism → medical emergency.
  • Bloody diarrhea → consider EHEC (E. coli O157:H7).
  • Pregnancy risk → Listeria (avoid soft cheeses/RTE meats unless well-heated).

9) Often-Missed Concepts (marks booster!)

  • Reheating ≠ fix for toxins: Staph. toxin survives normal reheating.
  • Vacuum risk: Low-oxygen packs need low pH/salt to prevent botulism.
  • Cooked rice danger: Cool rapidly; don’t keep warm for hours (B. cereus).
  • Listeria & fridge: Can grow at ≤ 4 °C; watch RTE meats/soft cheeses.
  • 2-hour/4-hour rule: Limit time in danger zone during prep/service.
  • High-risk groups: Pregnant, infants, elderly, immunocompromised.
  • Separate boards/knives: Raw poultry vs salads to avoid cross-contam.
  • Water safety: Contaminated water → fresh produce outbreaks.
  • Label & date: FIFO/FEFO reduces expired RTE risks.
  • Personal hygiene: Food handlers often spread Staph./Norovirus.
