1) What is Yeast?

Yeasts are **unicellular fungi** (single-celled eukaryotes), typically **3–10 µm**. They reproduce mainly by **budding** and can live with or without oxygen (**facultative anaerobes**). Famous species: Saccharomyces cerevisiae (baker’s/brewer’s yeast).

FeatureSimple meaningExam hook
Cell typeEukaryote (true nucleus)Has nucleus + organelles
ReproductionBudding (also fission)Bud = small outgrowth on parent
MetabolismFerments sugars to alcohol + CO₂Used in bread/wine/beer

2) History (very short)

Louis Pasteur (19th century) showed that yeast is a living organism that causes **alcoholic fermentation** — a key step in microbiology and food science.
YearMilestoneWhy it matters
1850s–1860sPasteur’s fermentation studiesProved yeast drives alcohol + CO₂
1876Hansen’s pure yeast culturesConsistent brewing/baking
20th c.Industrial yeast productionReliable baking and beverages
