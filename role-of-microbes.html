2) Why microbes in food?

  • Flavor & texture: organic acids, alcohol, CO₂, enzymes.
  • Safety: pH falls, bacteriocins (e.g., nisin) can suppress pathogens.
  • Shelf-life: acid/low aw slows spoilage microbes.
  • Nutrition: better digestibility, vitamin/peptide formation.
  • Economy: value addition (cheese, soy sauces, cocoa/coffee processing).

6) Safety & Control (simple plan)

Starter + Salt/Acid + Temperature + Hygiene = Safe Fermentation
  • Use starters (pure cultures) or healthy back-slopping.
  • Salt/Acid to select good microbes and speed acidification.
  • Temperature in correct range; avoid long “danger zone” (5–60 °C) when not intended.
  • Hygiene: clean vessels; avoid cross-contamination.
  • Packaging: oxygen control (vacuum/MAP) only with appropriate pH/salt.

9) Often-Missed Concepts (don’t forget!)

Concept1-line reminder
Wild vs Pure culturesWild gives unique flavors; pure cultures give consistency & safety.
Salt selectionRight % salt favors LAB and suppresses spoilers in pickles.
pH targetsReach low pH quickly for safety (many aim ≤ ~4.6).
Oxygen controlAcetobacter needs O₂ (vinegar); lactic ferments may be low-O₂.
Enzyme-led changesKoji enzymes create amino acids/peptides → umami.
Post-fermentation handlingCold storage slows over-acidification & spoilage.
Mycotoxins ≠ cellsHeating may kill moulds but not necessarily their toxins.
Toxico-infection vs intoxicationLive growth in gut vs pre-formed toxin in food.
Back-slop healthUse healthy, sour, pleasant previous batch only.
Hurdle mindsetCombine mild hurdles (salt, acid, temp, hygiene) for safe products.
